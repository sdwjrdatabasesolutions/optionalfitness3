Liberian Nurses at Phebe Hospital Prepare for Strike, Services Disrupted By SDWJR | TBA News Network

BONG COUNTY, LIBERIA — Operations at Phebe Hospital in Suakoko were significantly disrupted Friday as nurses and other staff began preparing for a strike expected to take place Monday, raising concerns about healthcare access across Bong County.

According to reports from the scene, several patients were instructed to leave their beds and continue treatment elsewhere as workers reduced services in anticipation of the demonstration. Some emergency cases were reportedly turned away, leaving families searching for medical help in neighboring counties. Relatives described confusion and distress as hospital personnel informed them that services would be scaled down ahead of the protest. The situation has intensified fears that the strike could severely impact healthcare delivery in central Liberia if a resolution is not reached quickly. The planned action by staff highlights growing tensions within the health sector and the pressure frontline workers say they are facing amid ongoing challenges in the system.
