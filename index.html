Suspect in Texas Bar Shooting Wore ‘Property of Allah’ Clothing and Iranian Flag Emblem, Source Says

By SDWJR | TBA News Network | March 2, 2026

AUSTIN, TEXAS — The gunman who killed two people and wounded 14 others at a crowded Austin bar early Sunday was wearing a sweatshirt that read “Property of Allah” and another shirt bearing an Iranian flag-style design, according to a law enforcement official who spoke to The Associated Press on condition of anonymity.[web:24][web:25]

Authorities have identified the suspect as 53-year-old Ndiaga Diagne, a naturalized U.S. citizen originally from Senegal, who was shot and killed by Austin police after the attack.[web:21][web:25] Investigators say Diagne first drove past the bar multiple times, then opened fire with a handgun from inside his SUV at people on the patio and in front of the establishment before stepping out with a rifle and continuing to shoot.[web:24][web:28]

The FBI and the Joint Terrorism Task Force are investigating the shooting as a possible act of terrorism, citing “indicators” found on the suspect and in his vehicle, including clothing with religious and Iranian imagery, an Iranian flag, and photos of Iranian leaders recovered from his home.[web:21][web:24][web:28] Officials emphasize that it is still too early to determine a definitive motive and are examining whether Diagne may have been self-radicalized or influenced by extremist ideology, while also reviewing reports of prior mental health episodes.[web:21][web:23]

The attack unfolded just before 2 a.m. outside Buford’s Backyard Beer Garden on Austin’s Sixth Street entertainment district, a nightlife hub lined with bars and music venues near the University of Texas.[web:24][web:28] Police and paramedics arrived within about a minute of the first 911 call, confronting and fatally shooting the gunman and rushing victims to area hospitals; several remain in critical condition.[web:21][web:28]

Federal and local officials are also weighing whether the timing of the shooting — one day after U.S. and Israeli forces launched a major attack on Iran — is significant, though they caution there is no confirmed operational link at this stage.[web:21][web:28][web:30] Austin’s mayor and university leaders have urged calm while offering support to victims and praising first responders, as investigators continue to gather witness statements, review surveillance and cellphone video, and analyze the suspect’s digital footprint.[web:21][web:23]

War Recap: Iran vs Israel and the United States

By SDWJR | TBA News Network | March 2, 2026

Iran, Israel, and the United States are now engaged in an open regional war that evolved from years of proxy clashes into direct military confrontation, with the latest phase marked by a coordinated U.S.–Israeli air campaign targeting key sites inside Iran.

The conflict’s roots trace back to an April 2024 Iranian drone and missile barrage on Israel and subsequent retaliatory strikes, followed by escalating clashes through 2024 and 2025 that drew the United States deeper into the fight; by early 2026, U.S. and Israeli forces were striking Iranian military and command targets as Iran and its allied militias launched rockets, drones, and missiles at Israel and U.S. facilities across the region, driving up casualties, destabilizing global markets, and leaving the endgame uncertain as Washington and Tel Aviv seek to degrade Iran’s military and regional influence while Tehran rejects negotiations and frames the war as a defense of sovereignty.

DHS releases Columbia University student

In an early morning raid that has ignited nationwide outrage, federal agents stormed a Columbia University dormitory and detained a 29-year-old neuroscience student in front of her roommate—allegedly under the false pretense of searching for a missing child. Elmina Aghayeva, a popular social media influencer from Azerbaijan, was handcuffed and taken into ICE custody after agents reportedly posed as NYPD officers using fake badges and a phony missing persons bulletin. Her crime? A visa status the government claims was terminated nearly a decade ago. Her ordeal—from a desperate Instagram plea to a dramatic White House intervention—raises urgent questions about how far federal power will reach into the lives of international students.

Hours after her detention, New York City Mayor Zohran Mamdani personally confronted President Trump during a White House meeting to demand her freedom. She was released by evening, but the battle is far from over. Columbia University is now accusing the Department of Homeland Security of using "false pretenses" to seize their student, while federal officials insist the arrest was lawful. As protests sweep the campus and immigrant communities brace for what comes next, TBA News Network investigates the tactics, the cover-up, and the student caught in the middle. About TBA News Network

TBA News Network is an independent, investigative media organization founded by journalist SDWJR. We were established to fill the critical void left by mainstream media—a space where stories of systemic injustice, institutional abuse, and violations of public trust often go untold. We operate on a simple but radical belief: journalism is most valuable when it challenges power, not when it protects it. We serve as a watchdog for the voiceless, providing a platform for fearless reporting on the issues that shape our world.

With a dedicated focus on civil rights, policing, governance, and corruption, TBA News Network digs deep into the systems that affect marginalized communities across the United States, Africa, and the global Black diaspora. We are not just reporters; we are truth-seekers committed to transparency. We prioritize the public interest over political access, corporate approval, or personal comfort. At TBA News Network, we don't just report the news—we investigate the truth behind it, amplifying underreported stories and voices excluded from dominant narratives.
