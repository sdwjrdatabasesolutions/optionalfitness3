Iran Confirms Security Chief Larijani and Basij Commander Soleimani Killed in Strikes

TEHRAN — The Iranian government has officially confirmed the deaths of two of its most powerful internal security figures: Ali Larijani, the longtime security chief and former parliament speaker who served as one of Supreme Leader Khamenei's most trusted advisors, and Gholamreza Soleimani, the commanding general of the Basij — Iran's feared internal paramilitary militia responsible for suppressing domestic dissent.

The confirmed killings come in the wake of coordinated U.S.–Israeli strikes that have now decapitated Iran's military and political command structure. Larijani's death removes a key architect of Iran's regional strategy, while the loss of Basij commander Soleimani is expected to send shockwaves through Iran's domestic security apparatus — the very force used to crush the 2019 and 2022 protest uprisings.

Iranian state media released a brief official statement confirming both deaths but offered no details on location or timing of the strikes. Western intelligence officials, speaking on condition of anonymity, described the dual eliminations as a decisive blow to Iran's command-and-control capability, leaving the Islamic Republic in an acute leadership vacuum at one of the most critical moments in its history.

About TBA News Network

TBA News Network is an independent, investigative media organization founded by journalist SDWJR. We were established to fill the critical void left by mainstream media — a space where stories of systemic injustice, institutional abuse, and violations of public trust often go untold. We operate on a simple but radical belief: journalism is most valuable when it challenges power, not when it protects it. We serve as a watchdog for the voiceless, providing a platform for fearless reporting on the issues that shape our world.

With a dedicated focus on civil rights, policing, governance, and corruption, TBA News Network digs deep into the systems that affect marginalized communities across the United States, Africa, and the global Black diaspora. We prioritize the public interest over political access, corporate approval, or personal comfort. At TBA News Network, we don't just report the news — we investigate the truth behind it.
